# CARMIX Consolidated Mega-Plan

Harden the shared base, then bank six composition stages plus one positioning doc. Every new stage reuses only committed primitives, touches no proven-core file, and ends with the standard pass/fail line plus an honest SCOPE block. The single machine-checked claim reused throughout is the anti-amplification monotonicity in kernel/proofs/Carmix.v that backs every cap re-mint. All demos are single-CPU or two-QEMU, software-only, with an emulated root of trust.

This plan incorporates the honesty critic. Five corrections are applied and called out where they land:
1. Audit framing downgraded: one real behavioral defect on plausible hardware (SMP index), the rest are latent guards or defense-in-depth, not a base riddled with bugs.
2. Front E renamed from "measurable superiority" to "architectural delta". Superiority is earned only by the break-even R* crossover and the exact byte-count storage wins, never by the strawman scoreboard.
3. run_platform demoted to optional and cut from the default queue. It re-runs what run_sr already banks. The positioning doc is the real deliverable.
4. "proof-carrying rematerialization" renamed to "witness-checked rematerialization". The witness is a gate re-execution, not a checkable offline proof object.
5. "validated on 2 real cores" reconciled to "two QEMU vCPUs exhibiting a real data race" wherever concurrency is claimed.

## THE EIGHT FRONTS

### FRONT G. Membrane-native graphics and interaction

Key design. The whole layer derives from one explicit membrane cut. FROZEN and content-addressed: a surface (pixel bytes), a window node (hash of surface-hash, rect, z, focus-flag, ordered child hashes), a scene root (one BLAKE3 over the ordered window-node DAG plus z-order), a captured input event (the 10-byte gfx_evt_t already hash-named), and the undo/redo history (an ordered list of scene-root hashes). LIVE and unnameable by any hash: current physical device state, wall-clock now, cursor position between captures, caret blink, animation timing, the vsync deadline, which key is held right now. The membrane crossing is exactly one operation, the input capture point: IRQ fires, read the register, stamp with a tick, store. Before the stamp the event is live and unnameable, after it is a frozen value re-minted to the focused surface cap. Three native consequences follow. Window management IS the anti-amp relation, not a WM: a window handle is a surface cap minted through the proven gate, a sub-window is a sub-rectangle cap with grant less than or equal to the parent, focus is a delegated input cap, and there is no privileged window-manager process. Damage, undo, crash-recovery, and session migration all collapse into rematerialize-by-hash: damage is the structural diff of two scene roots, undo is a jump to a prior scene-root hash re-presented (not replay), whole-desktop scrub is a run_sr time-jump over a scene payload. Usability is the history, not chrome: the desktop is a scrubbable content-addressed timeline where identical windows dedup and every painted rectangle carries the cap chain that minted it.

Only new capabilities. A recursive window-node DAG generalizing the current 2-leaf frame, sub-rect anti-amp caps, and scene-root time-travel. The present-skip in SCN-4 IS committed GFX-5, and scene dedup IS the committed store property. Do not sell the re-demonstrated store and gfx properties as new.

Buildable now. Scene as a content-addressed DAG with one root per frame and identical subtrees dedup by hash. Damage as structural diff with diff-proportional repaint reusing the committed prune-identical-subtrees walk. Window, sub-rect, and focus as anti-amp caps with over-grant refused by the same swcap_check as migration. Thin-multiplexer compositor with content-addressed present-if-changed skip. Captured event delivered as a re-mint bound to the focused surface cap, non-holder and forged over-authority delivery refused. Undo and redo as re-hash-confirmed jumps between scene-root hashes. Whole-desktop scrub as a run_sr root-jump with revocation-floored restore. Provenance log as the minting cap chain per painted node.

Needs research. A quantitative interactive-path cost model for where the content-address-versus-direct-blit crossover sits per input rate and dirty-tile size. A canonical form for scene equivalence so behaviorally equal but structurally different scenes could dedup (blocked by boundary 3.5). Anti-spoof labeling for visual spoofing inside a legitimately granted rect (caps bound who may paint, not what is painted, so this needs colored-border labeling). An energy-aware idle governor (needs joules QEMU does not model).

Build plan. Two serial stages after run_gfx. Front G build plan is items 6 and 7 in the serial queue below.

Honest boundary. Content-addressing conjures no drivers, fonts, toolkits, or apps. This front delivers the MODEL, not the ecosystem. The interactive path stays on the direct-blit fast path because hashing a full framebuffer at 60Hz is infeasible, so content-addressing lives only at coarse commit boundaries. QEMU headless framebuffer readback is the honest substitute for a physical monitor. Live input is synthesized and injected, and input liveness itself is never a stored object, only the captured value is. Caps stop injection and eavesdropping, not perception or spoofing inside a granted rectangle. Undo is bounded by GC retention. Time-travel obeys output-commit and cannot un-paint an already-externalized output. The trusted-path label is narrow: it binds who may paint, and must always print the anti-spoof caveat inline. Attestation inherits the emulated root. Scene equivalence is structural only.

### FRONT VC. Verifiable-computation primitives

Key design. One new CARMIX-native object, a content-addressed transition edge: store_put(pre_state, post_state, actor_taskhash, pre_ceilH, post_ceilH, parent_edge, kind). Everything else is composition. Cross-process is free because state and authority are already content-addressed, so an edge names states and an actor task-hash, not a PID, and two processes join when A's post_state appears as B's pre_state (a shared hash, no IPC). The lineage is an append-only DAG acyclic by hash-before-name, which is the operational content of the acyclicity property in kernel/proofs/Carmix.v, not a re-proof. The witness is a gate re-execution, not a new proof: a restored state travels with its edge, and the verifier re-checks without re-executing the computation by (i) re-hashing restored bytes to post_state, (ii) re-hashing the edge to its name, and (iii) re-minting post_ceilH from pre_ceilH through the swcap gate, which is a concrete instance of the same clamp-and-intersect function the Coq proof machine-checks as never-amplifying. Composition across a trust boundary re-mints the whole chain: a U-4 delegation chain becomes an edge-DAG, carried with an Ed25519 quote binding the DAG head and actor task-hash, re-minted per hop floor-bounded by revocation, accepted only if every hop attenuates end to end.

Naming correction (critic item 4). This is witness-checked rematerialization, not proof-carrying rematerialization. The witness is a re-execution of the gate that the verifier must trust, not a succinct offline proof object. The genuinely new primitive is the transition edge, which stands on its own without the "proof" label.

Buildable now. VC1 cross-process append-only transition-edge DAG with join by shared state name across two ring-3 processes, acyclic by hash-before-name, referential-integrity refusal on an unknown parent. VC2 witness-checked rematerialization by state re-hash plus edge re-hash plus one gate re-mint, with an amplifying-tamper negative control refused. VC3 verifiable cross-boundary delegation composition with an Ed25519 quote over the DAG head, receiver re-mints every hop floor-bounded by revocation, forged-quote and amplifying-hop and revoked-hop all rejected. Optional DET-edge re-derivation as the reproducibility check reusing the deterministic-spine versus external-edge distinction.

Needs research. A succinct proof object a distrusting third party verifies offline without re-executing the gate and without trusting the kernel (recursive SNARK, IVC, PCD, Nova-class). Correctness-of-computation in the witness (proving post is the intended function of pre, not merely a state reached by an authority-preserving edge). Machine-checking the model-to-C link down to the running gate (the CBMC rung and the seL4-style refinement, not crossed).

Build plan. One stage run_vc after run_sr and run_dbg. Front VC build plan is item 8 in the serial queue.

Honest boundary. The witness makes state and the authority relation trustlessly re-checkable by re-hash plus gate re-mint, but the verifier still trusts its own gate. Carmix.v machine-checks the abstract re_mint, not the running C. Across a trust boundary the actor's code identity still needs an attestation root, which is emulated. Cross-process means multiple ring-3 processes on one QEMU machine, not cross-machine. Acyclicity rides on hash-before-name creation order, not on BLAKE3 injectivity. It does not prove functional correctness of the step. run_vc banks a composition, upgrades no proof.

### FRONT E. Architectural delta (efficiency, not cross-OS superiority)

Renamed (critic item 2). Formerly "measurable superiority". run_bench measures the CARMIX-native path against a naive CARMIX in-kernel baseline (full-copy, no-dedup, raw register swap, memcpy-IPC), which is a self-constructed baseline, not a competing OS. The honest deliverable is the architectural delta plus one real crossover number. Superiority is earned only by the break-even reuse count R* and the exact byte-count storage wins. The WIN and LOSS verdicts against the naive baseline must never be quoted as "CARMIX beats X".

Key design. CARMIX prices everything through BLAKE3 anti-amplification. You pay one hash per unique object, and in return dedup, structural sharing, verifiable integrity, and time-travel are amortized free thereafter. Every efficiency claim reduces to one number, R*, plus one gate: is content-integrity a hard requirement. Above R* or when integrity is mandatory, the native path wins measurably. Below R* on cold, unique, trust-not-required data, it loses, and the harness must say so with equal prominence. Real wins: storage and memory via dedup and structural sharing, diff-proportional capture and migration, cross-process code sharing via dedup-by-hash, warm respawn as a dedup hit. Real losses stated plainly: cold unique object (BLAKE3 is pure overhead versus memcpy with no reuse), per-switch content-addressing (already proved to thrash, hashing dwarfs a register swap), IPC latency (a content-addressed message loses to memcpy shared memory). Per the Membrane Law a hash names content not liveness, so no latency win may be claimed on live interactive paths, only content-reuse wins are nameable.

Consolidation, not new measurement (critic item 2b). BENCH2 is R4, BENCH5(b) is the U3 thrash, BENCH1 is the CV5 dedup ratio. The only genuinely new artifacts are the break-even R* and the consolidated WIN/LOSS/TIE scoreboard. This is consolidation plus one new number, and must not read as new results.

Buildable now. run_bench as a pure consolidation stage over rdtsc, the store byte and dedup counters, the committed diff machinery, the U2 chunk-diff, the U3 register-swap timer, and the break-even shape. All seven sub-stages are wiring, not new mechanism.

Needs research. Physical-HW cycle numbers (all cycles are QEMU-TSC relative). Whether BLAKE3 has a hardware-accelerated path on the target CPU (SIMD or SHA-NI moves R* down). A defensible cross-OS claim (out of reach in-kernel, explicitly out of scope).

Build plan. One stage run_bench after run_int. Front E build plan is item 10 in the serial queue.

Honest boundary. Byte counts are exact ground truth from the store counters. Cycle counts are QEMU-TSC relative and must not be quoted as physical-HW timings. The baseline is a naive CARMIX in-kernel path, not another OS. Per the Membrane Law no latency win may be claimed on interactive paths. The harness must print its LOSS rows with the same prominence as its wins.

### FRONT SD. Capability-native software distribution

Key design. A package is identified and verified by content hash, its dependency closure is a Merkle DAG, install is store the closure plus grant a bounded capability set re-minted from the installer's ceiling, and it is reproducible-by-construction with capability-mediated composition from mutually distrusting sources. Everything is a composition of committed primitives. Package identity is the hash of a fixed-layout manifest object (the task-hash pattern). Dependency closure is the reachable Merkle DAG with edges as exact hashes, reachability and reclaim via the committed refcount oracle. Install-transfer is the diff-proportional migration between two store states with per-object re-hash verify, fail-closed on tamper. Capability-at-install is birth-time attenuation: the installer grants a bounded subset, a request for a cap it lacks is refused, and the granted region-cap is re-minted so destination authority is less than or equal to source. The installed system is a name-to-hash directory committed at a durable root, update re-roots (free versioning), rollback re-points the durable root O(1), uninstall is refcount decrement plus tombstone reclaim. Multi-source composition is the membrane join: each publisher signs its sub-graph root, ingest verifies the quote and re-hashes the sub-graph, a failing source drops to bottom and the composite still forms from the rest.

Why native not a Nix or OCI port (critic item 5, accepted). Store plus verify-on-read plus dedup plus GC plus the gate are proven kernel primitives, not a userland daemon over a trusted mutable filesystem. Content-addressing deletes the /usr namespace, which deletes the version conflict and the solver and the lockfile. Rollback is the absence of destruction, not its reversal. Two honest edges must never be softened: reproducible distribution not reproducible builds, and "which hash is current" needing a mutable trust-rooted index.

Buildable now. PKG-1 closure-as-Merkle-DAG with resolve-by-hash and fail-closed on a tampered edge. PKG-2 diff-proportional cross-store install with re-hash verify on land and dedup skip of shared deps. PKG-3 bounded cap grant, over-privileged install refused, re-mint through the proven gate. PKG-4 two coexisting libcore hashes with no solver plus cv_canon collapsing order-independent closures to one name. PKG-5 installed-set Merkle dir with durable switch ordered after content lands and O(1) rollback. PKG-6 uninstall via refcount decrement plus tombstone. PKG-7 two-publisher Ed25519 composition with a tampered source dropped and an unknown signer refused. PKG-8 rdtsc measurement.

Needs research. Real network fetch of a remote closure (loopback-only today). The global registry, discovery, and revocation index (where supply-chain trust re-concentrates, the real OPEN residue). Reproducible builds (no in-kernel compiler). Installed-set scale beyond the fixed 8-entry directory.

Build plan. One stage run_pkg after run_tools and before run_cv. Front SD build plan is item 5 in the serial queue.

Honest boundary. Content-addressing gives reproducible distribution, not reproducible builds. Coexisting hashes eliminate name conflicts, not ABI or semantic conflicts. "Which hash should I move to" needs a mutable trust-rooted index and is OPEN, the place supply-chain trust re-concentrates, because a hash names a fixed value not "the current best value". Real remote fetch is loopback-only. Use a run_pkg-local wider dir object, do not widen the shared FS_MAXENT. run_pkg must sit after userland init or grants refuse from an empty CSpace. No proven-core file is touched.

### FRONT TCB. Minimal trusted computing base

Key design. Anti-amplification is currently applied only outward (drivers, userland, disk, network) but never reflexively to the kernel's own subsystems. The trusted base splits three ways. Proven core: kernel/carmix/swcap.c (104 lines), whose theorems are closed by Qed in kernel/proofs/Carmix.v with nothing admitted, the only proven code. Irreducible TCB that cannot be proven away: BLAKE3 (it defines object identity, collision-resistance is the single explicit Hypothesis), the ring-0 MMU programmer (only ring 0 writes CR3 and page tables), the bootloader and firmware up to kmain, the baked first-authority key plus physical control of the raw store, and the custodian ROOT grants. Already outside the TCB and verified not trusted: every driver holds a bounded device cap minted through the proven gate, userland runs ring-3 with authority re-minted at every crossing, disk and network and migrated code are re-hashed on every load. The dominant honest fact: all of kernel/kernel.c runs in ring 0 in one translation unit, the proof covers 104 of those lines, and every stage has ambient authority that structurally bypasses the gate it demonstrates. The native move is to apply the proven device-cap path to the kernel's own subsystems so a bug in one subsystem cannot amplify past its cap.

Buildable now. TCB1 honest trusted-surface census printed on serial (proven 104, irreducible BLAKE3 plus mapper plus baked key, reducible remainder). TCB2 (load-bearing) hand one existing kernel arena a bounded cap via the proven mint-and-access path and show an out-of-arena write refused at the gate, reflexive anti-amplification on one subsystem. TCB3 post-mint tamper caught fail-closed. TCB4 collapse the run_sr signed attestation body to a 40-byte nonce plus body-root, all structure moved out to re-verifiable content-addressed land. TCB5 rdtsc cost of gated access versus raw pointer plus the irreducibility statement.

Needs research. Coq-to-C extraction or a verified-compilation link of the abstract re_mint onto swcap.c. Reflexive-gating the whole kernel so no stage retains ambient ring-0 authority (a multi-cycle rewrite, TCB2 only proves the pattern on one arena). A formally-verified minimal MMU mapper. A real hardware root of trust to replace the emulated key.

Build plan. One stage run_tcb after run_drv and run_sr. Front TCB build plan is item 9 in the serial queue.

Honest boundary. run_tcb shrinks and censuses the trusted base, it does not eliminate the irreducible floor and does not claim the whole kernel is gated. TCB2 is a witness on exactly one arena, not a rewrite of the single ring-0 translation unit. Irreducible and stated plainly: BLAKE3 correctness, the hardware-mandated MMU programmer, first-key custody plus physical store control, the boot chain to kmain, and the model-code gap between Carmix.v and swcap.c. TCB4 is real structure-shrink but the security stays emulated until a hardware root exists. No proof claim is upgraded.

### FRONT DIST. Distributed coherence as continuous migration

Key design. Distributed operation is migration generalized from a one-shot move into continuous bidirectional anti-entropy plus convergence plus attested cross-machine invocation. The load-bearing native claim: content-addressed state is a state-based CRDT (a join-semilattice) by construction, so convergent replication is free and needs no vector clocks on the immutable plane. The join is object-set union, commutative and associative because a hash carries no order and idempotent because dedup IS idempotence. Path-independence (two construction sequences, one identical name) is exactly the CRDT convergence law, already shown single-machine and under real concurrency across two QEMU vCPUs. What a CRDT lacks and CARMIX adds is the anti-amp gate bounding who may inject state, enforced across the boundary by the distrust attestation seam. The precise residue that is not a CRDT and is not free is the single mutable current-root pointer, which is unhashable by definition, needs consensus, and is exactly where CAP bites: the immutable object plane is AP and convergent and free, the root-selection plane is CP and needs consensus and is OPEN.

Concurrency phrasing correction (critic item 5). The two cores are QEMU vCPUs exhibiting a real data race, not physical hardware. Say "two QEMU vCPUs with a genuine data race", consistent with single-CPU/QEMU elsewhere.

Buildable now. DIST-0 port cv_canon into nettest.c so both machines share the byte-identical convergence routine (a genuine prerequisite, cv_canon is absent from nettest.c today). DIST-1 two-machine convergence, transfer a graph by the existing Merkle pull, both re-hash-verify and canonicalize, assert the two names are byte-identical. DIST-2 bidirectional anti-entropy, wire the reverse pull, reconcile disjoint stores to their union with diff-only bytes-on-wire, both converge to one name. DIST-3 attested cross-machine invocation bounded by re-mint, authorized ACCEPT, over-ceiling refused fail-closed, forged and unrecognized refused. DIST-4 print the CAP statement, object plane converges order-independently, current-root selection is the consensus residue with no content-addressed winner.

Needs research. Consensus for current-root selection under partition (Raft, Paxos, or a CRDT LWW-register, or permanently OPEN). Distributed consistent snapshot (Chandy-Lamport) for cluster-wide time-travel. Failure detection without a real clock.

Build plan. One stage run_dist in nettest.c only. Front DIST build plan is item 11 in the serial queue.

Honest boundary. Everything demonstrable is loopback and ivshmem between two QEMU instances sharing host memory, there is no NIC and no real transport and no external peer. Convergence is structural-state equivalence only. The anti-amp ceiling binds only the local endpoint, against a foreign peer only re-hash integrity is unilaterally enforceable. The attestation root is emulated. The single unhashable residue, which root is currently authoritative, needs consensus and is genuinely CAP-bound and OPEN. Convergence of state is banked, agreement on the pointer is not, and conflating the two is the overclaim to avoid.

### FRONT AUDIT. Code-quality and bug audit (read-only)

Framing downgraded (critic item 1). This is not a base riddled with bugs. It is one real behavioral defect on plausible hardware (SMP index, and even that is invisible under QEMU -smp 2), one coupled misattribution, two latent contract violations the committed boot never reaches, and one pure defense-in-depth assertion. Ship the latter three as one-line guards, not as a four-item stream of equal weight.

Findings, all verified against source.
- #1 MEDIUM, real-HW latent, QEMU-safe. kernel/kernel.c around 5860 to 5931. percpu[SMP_MAXCPU=8] is indexed by ACPI processor_id (Limine Processor UID, not dense) but bounded only by the loop count g_ncpu. A sparse or greater-than-8 UID makes percpu[idx] an out-of-bounds static write. QEMU -smp 2 hides it. Everything downstream (reverse-IPI attribution, cross-core dispatch, DRCC) trusts this mapping. Do first.
- #2 MEDIUM, coupled to #1. kernel/kernel.c around 5866 and 5872. lapic_index scans array positions and returns 0 on miss, so a non-contiguous UID's AP lands at a slot the scan never visits and IPIs are misattributed to CPU0, including a spurious IPI from an unknown LAPIC id.
- #3 LOW-MEDIUM, latent this boot. kernel/kernel.c around 6065. The GC3 root-set insert does e=gc_nrc++ without the 256-cap guard the shared apply path has, a one-past-end write if the table is full. Unreachable on the committed boot because the table stays far below the cap at GC3 time.
- #4 LOW, contract violation. kernel/kernel.c around 2800 and 2809. elf_parse uses unchecked 64-bit additions that wrap below len, bypassing the in-image guard. Reachable only via the trusted embedded image today, so it violates its own fail-closed contract without a live attacker path.
- #5 LOW, defense-in-depth. kernel/kernel.c around 1108. materialize writes to a dst read from the object with no check that the range lies inside the task's own stack. Safe today because the object comes from the trusted in-RAM store, so this is a comment-versus-check gap, not a reachable defect.

Buildable now. F-SMP1 (findings #1 and #2, one root-cause fix), F-GC1 (#3), F-ELF1 (#4), F-U1 (#5).

Needs research. Real-hardware validation of the SMP fix needs a machine or a crafted QEMU topology with non-contiguous or large ACPI UIDs, since QEMU -smp 2 cannot regress-test the broken path.

Build plan. Four surgical fixes, ordered first in the serial queue as items 1 to 4, each a localized guard reusing committed primitives.

Honest boundary. All five are grounded in current source lines and concrete failure inputs, but only #1 and #2 change observable behavior on plausible hardware, and #3, #4, #5 are latent on the present boot. They are real contract violations and one-past-end or arbitrary-write hazards, not observed crashes. The audit prioritized the reusable primitives and the every-boot SMP, GC, loader, and fault paths, not an exhaustive sweep of every stage.

### FRONT VISION. Foundational-platform synthesis

Key design. CARMIX is a next-era platform candidate because one 32-byte BLAKE3 name simultaneously does jobs Unix splits across a dozen unrelated subsystems: the integrity check, the dedup key, the diff and transfer unit, the rollback anchor, the migration referent, and, with authority folded into the hashed bytes, the capability ceiling. Three committed foundations: content-addressing (identity IS integrity, so dedup and diff-proportional transfer fall out of the model), anti-amplification re-mint (authority is a rebuildable bound re-minted through a fail-closed gate whose seven-permission monotonicity is machine-checked), and rematerialization (restore-by-hash as the one operation behind context switch, fault, load, migration, and time-travel, at coarse boundaries only). run_sr makes the composition concrete: eight leaves including both page-table-inclusive task hashes fold through the verified epoch tree into one root that names the whole live software system, time-travels a single component, and re-verifies under one signature.

Where it genuinely advances beyond Unix. Content-identity of live execution with diffable substructure (CRIU and VM images are opaque, ZFS and btrfs cover files not processes). Authority-coupled naming (differing-authority states cannot alias). An authority algebra with no maximal element (the only mint moves strictly down, relocating trust to explicit key custody plus physical store control rather than an always-live uid 0).

Where it honestly trails the art. No functional-correctness refinement (seL4 refines spec to C to binary, CARMIX proves an abstract re_mint and not that swcap.c refines it). No confinement or noninterference (EROS and seL4 have both, the largest single distance). No fine-grained memory safety (CHERI checks per load and store, swcap.c checks only at call sites). Nothing for the mutable data plane. No hardware breadth or real-time.

Deliverable. A positioning document that deduplicates the four existing docs into one honest "why next-era platform" narrative, upgrading no proof and adding no code. run_platform is cut from the default queue (critic item 3): it re-runs what run_sr already banks. Keep it only as an optional capstone if the queue later wants one end-to-end serial artifact.

Honest boundary. The vision front cannot upgrade any claim, it can only state the true standing. The machine-checked core is a seven-permission re_mint monotonicity lemma plus finite-tree acyclicity under one injectivity hypothesis, everything else is designed and tested against attack tables. All demos are single-CPU or two-QEMU, software-only. The System Root names the software object graph at an epoch boundary only, not device or DMA or firmware state, nor the instants between epochs. The root of trust is emulated. Do not let the machine-checked core's standing bleed onto the tested C. Present unification as coarse-boundary-only (refuted for fine-grained switching by the U3 crossover). "No ambient root" is an authority-algebra property, not "no trusted base".

## SERIAL KERNEL-BUILD QUEUE (dependency-ordered, the actionable output)

Harden the base first, then bank the composition stages. New stages add zero proven-core edits and reuse only committed primitives. Boot-order dependencies are load-bearing: UX consumes SCN roots and needs run_sr, VC needs run_sr and run_dbg, TCB needs run_drv and run_sr, run_pkg needs userland init.

**1. F-SMP1 (audit #1 + #2, one root cause).** Stage: fix in run_smp, ap_entry, lapic_index, isr_ipi (no new stage). Stop keying percpu by ACPI processor_id, assign a dense slot 0..g_ncpu-1 in the setup loop, carry it to ap_entry via the Limine MP extra_argument, guard idx<SMP_MAXCPU at every write, make lapic_index return -1 on miss, guard isr_ipi on idx>=0. Reuses: existing lapic and percpu structs, the Limine MP extra_argument, the run_smp print sites. Seams: S1 and S2 print (processor_id, lapic_id, dense-slot) per core and assert dense-slot<g_ncpu and lapic_index(lapic_id)==slot, S3 forward and reverse IPI then attribute to the correct core, a spurious IPI from an unknown id is rejected not charged to CPU0. Boundary: only regress-testable under QEMU dense UIDs unless a crafted -smp topology is added. Do first because it is the only finding that changes behavior on plausible hardware and cross-core dispatch and DRCC trust this mapping.

**2. F-GC1 (audit #3).** Stage: fix at kernel.c ~6065 in run_gc GC3. Replace the raw e=gc_nrc++ insert with the guarded path (call gc_rc_apply then find, or replicate its if(gc_nrc>=256) return). Reuses: the committed gc_rc_apply bound check. ## HONEST MEASURABLE-SUPERIORITY CLAIMS

Earned and defensible.
- Storage and memory: K near-identical versioned images cost base plus K times the delta versus K times full for the naive path, an exact byte-count win from the store counters that grows with reuse and with similarity.
- Diff-proportional capture and migration: a localized change re-stores only the dirty set, cost tracks the dirty set not the state size, measured by the committed diff machinery (1-leaf to 1-object).
- Cross-process code sharing: N images sharing code store it once, savings codesize times N-1, exact from the dedup counters.
- Warm respawn: a second identical image is a dedup hit with 0 new bytes.
- Break-even R*: the one real crossover number, R* = hash-plus-store cyc over naive per-reuse cyc saved. Above R* or when integrity is mandatory the native path wins, below R* on cold unique data it loses.

Explicit non-wins, stated with equal prominence.
- Cold unique object: BLAKE3 is pure overhead versus memcpy with no reuse and no integrity need.
- Per-switch content-addressing: hashing one chunk dwarfs a register swap at every size (already measured to thrash).
- IPC latency: a content-addressed message loses to memcpy shared memory, the win is integrity and dedup-of-repeats and delegation, never latency.

Guardrails. Byte counts are exact ground truth. Cycle counts are QEMU-TSC relative and not physical-HW timings. The baseline is a naive CARMIX in-kernel path, not another OS, so no cross-OS "beats X" claim is made. Per the Membrane Law no latency win is claimed on live interactive paths. CRIU, ZFS, and btrfs comparisons are positioning, never measured results.

## FOUNDATIONAL-POSITIONING PARAGRAPH

CARMIX is a next-era platform candidate because one 32-byte BLAKE3 name simultaneously does jobs Unix splits across a dozen unrelated subsystems: it is the integrity check, the dedup key, the diff and transfer unit, the rollback anchor, the migration referent, and, with authority folded into the hashed bytes, the capability ceiling. The thesis rests on three committed foundations. Content-addressing means identity IS integrity, so dedup and diff-proportional transfer fall out of the model rather than a separate pass. Anti-amplification re-mint means authority is a rebuildable bound over content-addressed state re-minted through a fail-closed gate whose seven-permission monotonicity is machine-checked in kernel/proofs/Carmix.v. Rematerialization means restore-by-hash is the one operation behind context switch, fault, load, migration, and time-travel, at coarse boundaries only. run_sr makes the composition concrete: eight leaves including both page-table-inclusive task hashes fold through the verified epoch tree into one root that names the whole live software system, time-travels a single component, and re-verifies under one signature. Where it genuinely advances beyond Unix: content-identity of live execution with diffable substructure (CRIU and VM images are opaque, ZFS and btrfs cover files not processes), authority-coupled naming so differing-authority states cannot alias, and an authority algebra with no maximal element where the only mint moves strictly down, relocating trust to explicit key custody plus physical store control rather than an always-live uid 0. Where it honestly trails the art: no functional-correctness refinement (seL4 refines spec to C to binary, CARMIX proves an abstract re_mint and not that swcap.c refines it), no confinement or noninterference (EROS and seL4 have both, the largest single distance), no fine-grained memory safety (CHERI checks per load and store, swcap.c checks only at call sites), nothing for the mutable data plane, and no hardware breadth or real-time. The root of trust is emulated, all demos are single-CPU or two-QEMU and software-only, and only 104 lines are proven, so the machine-checked core's standing must never bleed onto the tested C. The Membrane Law is the organizing principle that sorts every future feature into what a hash can name (frozen, content-addressed, dedupable, time-travelable) and what it cannot (the live edge, wall-clock now, observable behavior, physical genuineness), and it must never convert a current gap into a virtue. This is not a Linux, Unix, or Windows port: the unifying noun is rematerialization of content-addressed live execution, where Unix unifies through files and Plan 9 through namespaces, and conventional elements (Ed25519 for the ceiling signature, a standard bootloader, an epoch Merkle tree) are used only as carriers of the content-addressed thesis, not as the thesis.
